19/11/2020

Meaning

Does a tree have meaning? And if so, what is its meaning?

How about a giraffe? Or maybe an ape?

Do you believe people differ enough from trees, giraffes, or apes to have a different meaning?

How so?

Who decides what our meaning is, what force governs the existence of meaning?

If there is a greater force to which we owe our meaning, how different, to this being, are we from a tree? Are we more deserving of meaning than our planetary kin? Are humans special in the scope of everything?

If so, is our meaning objective? If so; we all share a unified meaning, so then to look for individual meaning would be the same as looking towards the meaning of humanity. So then look to your neighbour and try to find what you have in common, and find his neighbour, and his, and so forth, and find that which we all have or could have in common, in ideas or goals. That should be our meaning.

Is meaning subjective? Who then governs over this meaning? Who decides or creates this meaning? Is it an external force? If so, I refer to my earlier statement about how an external force would see trees, or apes as different enough to make the exception to us humans to give us meaning and deny that of a tree. Or if they do have meaning why ours would be any different? And if you then were to answer that the external force decides a different meaning for all thing and beings, and then proceeds to withhold that information, then, obviously, it does not what you to find out about it.

Now if it is not an external force then that should mean it is yourself whom decides your own meaning. Which means that you govern your own fulfilment of the meaning if you believe in free will. If not, then it goes back to being an external force, whether it be society, kin or the supernatural. Which the n brings up the same questions as previously stated.

I think our meaning is the same as that of a tree.
